Structure theory

Last updated: October 15, 2005

The first main goal of the Atlas software package is to make available all the structural data about reductive groups that is required for representation theory. Let G be a connected complex reductive algebraic group, with a fixed chosen inner class. The following structural data are currently available for G :

  • the computation of the component group of any real form of G;
  • the classification of weak and strong real forms for G;
  • the classification of conjugacy classes of Cartan subgroups of G;
  • the computation of the real Weyl group corresponding to each Cartan subgroup;
  • the classification of the K-orbits in G ⁄ B;

It should be stressed that all this information is computed, not looked up in databases. The only input that the program requires is the root datum for G (which is constructed during the initial user interaction), and the choice of inner class.
